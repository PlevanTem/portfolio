Reafel

"Frail elderly patients are able to receive specialized advice from heart physicians without exhausting hospital visits."

Type:Course work; Duration:4 weeks; role:UX designer | UX researcher; Tool: Figma


Background


Atrial fibrillation(AF or AFib) is the most frequent cardiac arrhythmia. All-cause mortality was highest in diagnosed AF patients, far exceeding the rate of stroke or major bleeding. Early detection of cardiac rhythm disorders is highly important for timely intervention and prevention of serious conditions such as stroke and repeated falls.






Design Process






Market Research


During this session, I came cross the atrial fibrillation topic, as it’s one of the major cardiovascular epidemics in the 21st century, causing ischaemic strokes, heart failure, cardiovascular deaths, and unplanned hospitalizations. Thus, I searched some facts on literatures, news reports and more.


Conclusion

  • Huge and increasing AFib healthcare need for middle-aged and seniors group
  • AFib risk increases with age
  • The prevalence of Male is doubled than female




Problem Framing



How might we provide an easy and convenient way to monitor AF patients and reduce doctors’ burden of routine checks?




Proposed system






Patient-End


Reafel

Reafel helps elderly AFib patients build better symptom management.

  • Set up monitor and log symptom easily
  • Track heart health and predict AFib risk by AI
  • Get advice from clinicians without exhausting hospital visits




Old-friendly symptom log


Seniors don’t like sophisticated procedures. Natural interaction is easier for them. Voice assistant and chatbot are provided for better accessibility and improve log efficiency.




Heart health on sight


Keep track of your heart health and get reminded. You can be relieved to live a proper daily habits. Professional suggestions are provided for healthier AFib lifestyle.

Clinician-End


Reafel makes it easier to prioritise patients, process their symptoms and have mutual communication. Also, AI helps highlight abnormal heart rhythms, which reduces clinicians's eye burden from tedious ECG data.




Iterations


User Story Map


To understand development goals and the context of each story(what, how, and why), we structured user stories and mapped by users' roles and considered their goals, activities and tasks.

UX workflow evaluation


How might data visulization helps user ensure heart health?

In this iteration, we validated the visualisations present in both the patient and the healthcare professional apps through qualitative empirical testing, using the “Thinking aloud” method. For the patient prototype, we tested two versions of the heart monitoring history present in the home page.
